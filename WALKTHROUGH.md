## 2. Position sizing — the single most important part

The goal is to **survive the moonshots going to zero** while still catching the winners. Suggested split of your pot by risk bucket:

| Bucket | % of pot | On £5,000 | What it's for |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Steady** (leaders) | 40% | £2,000 | Core, sleep-at-night holdings |
| **Balanced** (quality growth) | 30% | £1,500 | Main growth engine |
| **Aggressive** | 20% | £1,000 | Higher-risk, real businesses |
| **Moonshot** | 10% | £500 | Lottery tickets — assume you could lose it all |

**Per-name cap:** no single stock > ~8–10% of the pot. For moonshots, keep each to ~1–2%. With ~35 names you do **not** buy all of them — pick **8–15** you actually understand.

---

## 3. When to invest — don't go all-in on day one

- **Drip in (pound-cost averaging).** Split your entry into 3–4 buys over a few weeks/months. This protects you from buying the exact top.
- **Buy the thesis, not the hype spike.** If a name just jumped 40% on news, wait for it to settle.
- **Keep ~10–20% cash** so you can buy dips and new ideas the weekly scan surfaces.

---

## 4. How long to hold (matches the "Horizon" tag)

- **Long (3–5y+):** leaders & structural themes (NVDA, GEV, TSM, OKLO). Ride volatility; review quarterly, don't react to noise.
- **Medium (1–3y):** growth mid-caps — give the story time, re-assess each earnings.
- **Short (weeks–months):** catalyst/momentum trades — set a target and a stop before you buy, and actually obey them.

---

## 5. What to watch (red flags & exits)

- **Dilution / cash burn** — common killer for speculative small-caps. Repeated share issuance = your slice shrinks.
- **Customer concentration** — many AI names lean on 1–2 giant customers; losing one is catastrophic.
- **Valuation vs. analyst targets** — several names here trade far above targets; sentiment can reverse hard.
- **Policy/geopolitics** — clean energy (subsidies), TSMC (Taiwan), defence (budgets).
- **Have an exit rule:** decide in advance the % loss where you sell a speculative name (e.g. −30–40%), and consider trimming winners that balloon past your per-name cap.
