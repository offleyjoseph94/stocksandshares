Where the world is going — the genres I'm betting on

This is the "brain": the structural shifts that decide which sectors do well over the next 1–10 years. Every stock below sits inside one of these.

1 · AI compute & infrastructure

The single biggest capital cycle of the decade. AI models need chips, networking, memory and data centres. The surest money is often the "picks & shovels" — the suppliers everyone must buy from — rather than guessing which AI app wins.

2 · Energy for AI & electrification

AI data centres and EVs are exploding electricity demand. The grid, gas turbines and especially nuclear / SMRs become essential. Power is the bottleneck for AI — so power is an AI trade.

3 · Clean energy & batteries

Solar, storage and next-gen batteries keep scaling on cost, even through policy noise. More cyclical and policy-sensitive, so this is a buy-the-leaders-plus-a-few-moonshots genre.

4 · Defence & space

Rising geopolitical tension = sustained rearmament budgets. Drones, missiles, satellites and "new space" are multi-year tailwinds, increasingly favouring domestic (US-made) suppliers.

5 · Quantum & frontier compute

Pure moonshot. Tiny revenue, huge narrative. Could be transformational or could stall for a decade — size positions accordingly.

6 · Robotics & automation

Labour costs + AI brains = the long arc toward physical automation (delivery, logistics, humanoids). Early innings, high risk.

7 · Fintech & digital money

Stablecoins, real-time payments and neobanks are eating traditional finance. Regulation is the swing factor — clarity is bullish.

8 · Frontier biotech

Precision oncology, GLP-1s and gene therapy. Binary by nature — single trial readouts move stocks 50%+. Treat each as a lottery ticket with a thesis.
