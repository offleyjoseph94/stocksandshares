# 2026-06-25 — Cycle 1 deep dive (12 stocks)

This week's deep dive covers 12 stocks spread across all six watchlist genres: **AI & Compute** (NVDA, TSM), **Energy & Nuclear** (GEV, CCJ), **Clean & Batteries** (FSLR, ENPH), **Defence & Space** (KRMN, RDW), **Quantum** (IONQ, QBTS), and **Fintech & Other** (CRCL, KLAR).

## NVDA — Nvidia

**What it does:** Nvidia designs GPUs and full-stack AI computing systems (chips, networking via Mellanox/NVLink/Spectrum, and CUDA software). It is fabless — manufacturing is outsourced almost entirely to TSMC. Revenue is dominated by the Data Center segment (AI training/inference GPUs sold to hyperscalers, cloud providers, and enterprises), with smaller Gaming (GeForce), Professional Visualization, and Automotive segments. It increasingly sells full "AI factory" systems (racks, networking, software) rather than standalone chips.

**Latest results & financial health:** Q1 FY2027 (quarter ended April 26, 2026, reported May 20, 2026): record revenue of ~$81.6bn, up 85% YoY and 20% sequentially; Data Center revenue ~$75.2bn (+92% YoY). GAAP/non-GAAP gross margin ~74.9%/75.0%. GAAP/non-GAAP diluted EPS ~$2.39/$1.87. Free cash flow was a record ~$49bn for the quarter. Balance sheet is very strong — debt-to-equity around 0.05, expansion funded mainly from internal cash. Guidance for Q2 FY2027 (quarter ending ~July 2026) was ~$91bn, above the ~$86.8bn consensus. The company also announced an $80bn incremental buyback and raised its quarterly dividend to $0.25/share. Note: prior-year comparisons are muddied by a $4.5bn H20 inventory charge taken in FY2026 tied to China export curbs.

**Bull thesis:** Dominant supplier of AI training/inference compute with a deeply entrenched CUDA software moat; demand visibility extends through the Blackwell-to-Rubin transition; record backlog and guidance suggest no near-term AI capex slowdown among hyperscalers; expanding into networking/systems increases dollar content per AI cluster; margins remain exceptionally high alongside huge FCF generation funding buybacks/dividends.

**Key catalysts ahead:** Q2 FY2027 earnings expected late August 2026; ramp of the Vera Rubin platform (unveiled CES 2026, six-chip platform claiming up to 10x inference token-cost reduction vs. Blackwell) expected to start contributing revenue in H2 calendar 2026; resolution of the stalled H200/China shipment review; continued hyperscaler capex commentary each earnings season.

**Main risks:** China policy risk is the most concrete — over $20bn of annual sales exposure if Beijing or Washington fully blocks H20/H200 sales, and shipments are currently stalled pending a State Department review; customer concentration among a handful of hyperscalers who could pull back capex or design custom silicon (Google TPU, Amazon Trainium); competitive threats from AMD (MI series), custom ASICs, and potentially Intel; valuation risk if AI capex growth decelerates; supply chain dependency on TSMC for leading-edge nodes.

**Competitive position:** Still the clear AI compute leader by a wide margin; AMD is the main merchant-silicon competitor but trails significantly in AI accelerator share; hyperscaler custom chips (Google TPU, Amazon Trainium/Inferentia, Microsoft Maia) are a rising but still secondary threat; Nvidia's CUDA ecosystem and networking stack remain key differentiators.

**Rough valuation sense-check:** Trailing P/E roughly ~30-31x as of June 2026, forward P/E roughly ~23x — notably below Nvidia's own 3-year average (~52x) and 12-month average (~46x), reflecting how much earnings have grown into the price.

## TSM — Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Co (ADR)

**What it does:** TSMC is the world's largest dedicated semiconductor foundry (pure-play contract manufacturer), making chips designed by customers like Nvidia, Apple, AMD, and Qualcomm rather than selling its own branded products. High-Performance Computing (AI/data center chips) is now its largest and fastest-growing revenue segment, ahead of smartphone chips.

**Latest results & financial health:** Q1 2026 (reported April 2026): consolidated revenue of NT$1,134.1bn (~US$35.9bn), above guidance; revenue up 35.1% YoY and 8.4% sequentially. Net income ~NT$572.5bn, up 58.3% YoY; diluted EPS ~US$3.49/ADR unit. Gross margin ~66.2%, operating margin ~58.1%, net margin ~50.5%. Q2 2026 guidance is $39.0-40.2bn, with management reiterating full-year 2026 USD revenue growth "above 30%." HPC/AI-related revenue made up roughly 55% of Q4 2025 net revenue. TSMC has historically run a net-cash, investment-grade balance sheet, but exact Q1 2026 cash/debt figures should be checked against the primary 6-K filing.

**Bull thesis:** Effective monopoly on the most advanced logic nodes (3nm/2nm) used in AI accelerators and flagship smartphones; pricing power is rising as it becomes indispensable to both Nvidia and Apple; AI-driven HPC demand is structurally outgrowing smartphones; geographic diversification (Arizona, Japan, Germany fabs) reduces some geopolitical tail risk over time while leading-edge production stays in Taiwan by law.

**Key catalysts ahead:** Q2 2026 earnings (typically mid-July); continued 2nm node ramp amid reported capacity constraints; Arizona Fab 21 Phase 2 tool installation through 2026 ahead of H2 2027 volume production; ongoing US-Taiwan tariff/trade negotiations.

**Main risks:** Taiwan-China geopolitical risk is existential and cannot be hedged away; Taiwan law currently bars TSMC from producing its most advanced nodes abroad; customer concentration is real (top 10 customers ~76% of revenue, Nvidia ~22%, Apple ~18%); US export-control policy shifts affect TSMC indirectly through its largest customers; Arizona/overseas expansion carries a structurally lower margin profile than Taiwan fabs.

**Competitive position:** Dominant and arguably widening its lead at the leading edge — Samsung Foundry (~12% global share) has struggled to win major external customers, and Intel Foundry remains far behind in scale and yield at advanced nodes.

**Rough valuation sense-check:** ADR trading around $467-468 as of June 22, 2026; trailing P/E roughly 38-41x (well above its 10-year historical average of ~22x), forward P/E roughly ~28x.

## GEV — GE Vernova

**What it does:** GE Vernova was spun off from General Electric in April 2024 and operates across three segments: Power (gas turbines, steam, nuclear, hydro), Wind (onshore/offshore turbines), and Electrification (grid equipment, software). It sells and services power generation equipment, with a large, high-margin recurring services/parts business layered on top of long-cycle equipment sales. The 2026 acquisition of full ownership of Prolec GE (transformers) deepened its electrification exposure.

**Latest results & financial health:** Q1 2026 revenue rose ~16-17% YoY to ~$9.3bn, with 7% organic growth (reported April 2026). Net income hit ~$4.7bn, but this was heavily inflated by a $4.0bn pre-tax non-cash gain from remeasuring its prior Prolec GE stake plus a $330m gain on selling its Proficy software business — underlying operating profitability is much smaller than the headline suggests. Free cash flow was ~$4.8bn (more than 4x YoY). Orders were $18.3bn (+71% YoY), pushing backlog from $116bn to $163bn. Cash balance ~$10.2bn, investment-grade balance sheet. FY2026 guidance raised to $44.5-45.5bn revenue, 12-14% adjusted EBITDA margin, $6.5-7.5bn free cash flow.

**Bull thesis:** Sits at the center of two structural demand waves — global gas turbine/grid buildout and AI-driven data center electricity demand. Power segment orders grew 59% with margin expansion; electrification data-center orders alone hit $2.4bn in Q1, already exceeding all of 2025. Gas turbine slot reservations grew from 83 GW to 100+ GW. A ~2x book-to-bill ratio signals years of revenue visibility.

**Key catalysts ahead:** Continued AI/hyperscaler power deals; progress toward management's stated $200bn backlog target by 2027; gas turbine slot reservations reaching a targeted 110+ GW by year-end 2026; quarterly earnings expected ~late July 2026.

**Main risks:** Wind segment remains structurally loss-making (~$382m EBITDA loss in Q1 2026) amid offshore wind cost overruns and policy uncertainty; reported net income is distorted by one-off gains, masking true run-rate earnings; US tariffs on steel, copper, and components threaten margins; long-cycle equipment business carries project delay/cost-overrun risk; valuation already prices in years of growth.

**Competitive position:** Competes with Siemens Energy/Siemens Gamesa, Vestas, Mitsubishi Power, Westinghouse, Framatome, and Rolls-Royce depending on segment. Gas turbine and grid franchise is best-in-class for the AI power buildout, but the wind business lags peers on profitability.

**Valuation sense-check:** Trailing P/E reported around 32-33x, roughly a 48% premium to its sector median — reflecting enthusiasm for AI-power exposure rather than current (gain-distorted) earnings.

## CCJ — Cameco

**What it does:** Cameco is one of the world's largest uranium producers, mining and selling uranium (notably from Cigar Lake and the Inkai joint venture in Kazakhstan, 40% interest) primarily under long-term contracts with nuclear utilities. Since November 2023 it has also co-owned (49%, with Brookfield at 51%) Westinghouse Electric, diversifying revenue into reactor services, fuel, and new-build/SMR technology.

**Latest results & financial health:** Q1 2026 (reported ~May 2026): revenue figures vary by source (~$607m-$845m — treat precise number as unverified pending primary filing); net earnings attributable to equity holders were ~$131m (+87% YoY), adjusted net earnings more than doubled to ~$203m, adjusted EBITDA rose ~44% to ~$509m. Average realized uranium price was ~$66.21/lb vs ~$62.55/lb a year earlier. As of year-end 2025, ~$1.2bn cash/short-term investments against ~$1.0bn total debt (fully repaid its US term loan) — a solid, deleveraging balance sheet. FY2026 guidance reaffirmed.

**Bull thesis:** Structural uranium supply deficit after years of underinvestment in mine development; nuclear is a primary beneficiary of AI/data-center electricity demand and energy-security policy; Westinghouse stake provides growing distribution income (~$49m distribution in early 2026) and SMR/new-build optionality; ~15 new reactors expected online globally in 2026 (~12 GW).

**Key catalysts ahead:** Continued Westinghouse distributions and updates on its new-build/SMR pipeline (e.g. Dukovany project); further long-term contracting given utility restocking; China's Linglong One SMR startup (H1 2026); next quarterly results ~July/August 2026.

**Main risks:** Heavy commodity-price exposure to uranium spot/contract prices; geopolitical/supply concentration risk given reliance on Kazakhstan (Inkai) and the broader market's dependence on Russian enrichment capacity (~40-46% of global share); Kazatomprom cutting 2026 guidance by 10% shows supply-side can swing both ways; valuation looks stretched on trailing earnings.

**Competitive position:** Competes primarily with Kazatomprom (~23% global output) and Orano (France); Cameco differentiates via Tier-1 Canadian assets, Western-jurisdiction supply chain appeal, and the unique Westinghouse reactor-services platform.

**Valuation sense-check:** Trailing P/E reported around 100-113x as of June 2026 (vs its own ~84x trailing 12-month average) — trading well above its already-elevated recent historical norm.

## FSLR — First Solar

**What it does:** First Solar designs and manufactures solar PV modules using proprietary thin-film cadmium telluride (CdTe) technology rather than conventional crystalline silicon. It sells almost exclusively to utility-scale and large commercial solar developers in the US and select international markets, signing multi-year forward sale agreements.

**Latest results & financial health:** Q1 2026 (reported April 30, 2026) net sales were ~$1.04bn, up 24% YoY; net income $347m ($3.22 diluted EPS, beating consensus ~$2.87) vs $210m a year earlier. Gross profit ~$486m (up 41% YoY); adjusted EBITDA $520m vs $379m. Full-year 2025 EPS was $14.21. FY2026 guidance reaffirmed: net sales $4.9-5.2bn, EPS ~$18.00, gross profit $2.4-2.6bn, module shipments 17-18.2 GW. Balance sheet strong: ~$2.43bn cash against ~$587m debt (net cash ~$1.84bn), debt/equity of just 0.03.

**Bull thesis:** Near-monopoly position in utility-scale CdTe thin-film with a largely US-based, IRA-advantaged manufacturing footprint (~14 GW US capacity by 2026), insulating it from tariff/FEOC restrictions hitting Asian-supply-chain rivals. Strong contracted backlog via multi-year forward sales gives revenue visibility. Has already monetized over $2bn of Section 45X manufacturing tax credits.

**Key catalysts ahead:** Q2 2026 guidance of 3.4-4.0 GW shipments and $400-500m adjusted EBITDA; continued 45X credit monetization; new AD/CVD petition against solar imports from India, Indonesia and Laos (preliminary determination expected mid-2026, potential duties of 30-280%); Section 232 polysilicon national-security probe. Section 201 tariffs themselves expired February 2026.

**Main risks:** Heavy reliance on US clean-energy policy stability (IRA/45X credits, domestic content rules); technology risk from crystalline-silicon TOPCon/HJT efficiency gains eroding CdTe's edge; customer concentration in utility-scale demand tied to interconnection queues and financing costs; trade-case outcomes could cut both ways.

**Competitive position:** Globally a small player by volume (~2% module market share) against Chinese giants Jinko, LONGi, Trina and JA Solar (each ~11-13% share), but First Solar's edge is structural — a protected US manufacturing base and a product suited to high-temperature utility sites.

**Rough valuation sense-check:** Trailing P/E roughly 12-13x (down from ~18x in Q4 2025), market cap ~$27.7bn — below broader market multiples and arguably cheap if FY2026 guidance ($18 EPS) is hit.

## ENPH — Enphase Energy

**What it does:** Enphase makes microinverter-based solar energy systems plus home battery storage (IQ Battery line), EV chargers, and energy management software, sold mainly into the US residential rooftop solar market with growing exposure to Europe and commercial/three-phase systems.

**Latest results & financial health:** Q1 2026 (reported late April 2026) revenue was $282.9m, with ~1.41m microinverters and 103 MWh of batteries shipped; adjusted EPS of $0.47 beat the $0.44 consensus; free cash flow $83m. Non-GAAP gross margin ~44%, non-GAAP operating margin ~17%. GAAP gross margin slipped to ~44.2% from ~46.6% the prior quarter. Q2 2026 guidance: revenue $280-310m (including ~$85m of "safe-harbor" revenue) and battery shipments of 100-110 MWh. Balance sheet: ~$930.6m cash vs ~$612-1,244m debt depending on measure (net cash roughly $318m).

**Bull thesis:** Dominant brand and installer familiarity in US residential solar — Enphase and SolarEdge together hold over 80% of the US residential inverter market, and Enphase's IQ Battery 5P plus Tesla Powerwall 3 together account for over 65% of US residential storage installations. New US-manufactured commercial product (IQ9N-3P) helps customers meet domestic-content/FEOC requirements.

**Key catalysts ahead:** Continued IQ9 microinverter rollout for global residential markets and IQ Battery 5th-generation launch; commercial channel expansion off the IQ9N-3P; channel inventory normalization (guided to under-ship ~$25m in Q2 to correct Q1 over-shipping); clarity on US residential demand post the 2025 tax-credit pull-forward effect fading.

**Main risks:** US residential solar demand has weakened sequentially following expiration/reduction of federal residential solar tax incentives; reciprocal tariffs are squeezing gross margin; channel inventory overhang adds near-term revenue lumpiness; higher leverage than First Solar; intensifying competition from Tesla Powerwall 3 and lower-cost entrants like EG4 PowerPro.

**Competitive position:** Co-leader with SolarEdge in US residential inverters; in storage, Tesla Powerwall 3 competes aggressively on price/capacity per unit and FranklinWH offers more scalable stacking. Ecosystem lock-in (existing microinverter installs feeding battery attach) is the core moat.

**Rough valuation sense-check:** Trailing P/E around 36-56x depending on source (up from ~24x in Q4 2025), forward P/E roughly 28x — a substantial premium to First Solar and the market.

**Catalysts ahead:** Q2 2026 earnings (likely Aug 2026); ramp of the $1bn+ contingent demand commitments into firm orders; integration progress on Seemann/MSC; potential further hypersonics program awards.

## IONQ — IonQ

**What it does:** IonQ builds trapped-ion quantum computers, sold via cloud access (AWS, Azure, Google Cloud) and direct enterprise/government contracts, plus an emerging quantum networking business. Trapped-ion technology uses individually controlled ions as qubits, argued to give higher gate fidelity than superconducting approaches at the cost of slower gate speeds. In 2025-2026 IonQ went on an acquisition spree to vertically integrate: Oxford Ionics ($1.075bn, closed Sept 2025), Lightsynq and ID Quantique/Qubitekk (photonic networking), Vector Atomic (quantum sensing), and a pending ~$1.8bn deal for SkyWater Technology (chip fab, expected to close Q2-Q3 2026).

**Latest results & financial health:** Q1 2026 revenue was ~$64.7m, up roughly 755% YoY and beating consensus (~$49.7m) by about 30%. Record RPO/backlog of ~$470m, up 554% YoY. Still deeply unprofitable: non-GAAP EPS loss of $0.34/share and adjusted EBITDA of roughly -$96.75m. Full-year 2025 revenue was ~$130m (+202% YoY). FY2026 guidance raised to a ~$260-270m midpoint range after Q1. Cash, equivalents and investments of roughly $3.1bn as of March 31, 2026, no meaningful debt, though GAAP earnings are heavily distorted by warrant/derivative fair-value gains (~$1.06bn gain cited in Q1 2026) — ongoing dilution risk from stock-funded M&A and equity issuance.

**Bull thesis:** Fastest revenue growth among pure-play quantum names, large cash cushion buys years of runway, an aggressive build-out of a vertically integrated stack (chips, networking, sensing) could create a moat, and management has repeatedly beaten and raised guidance.

**Catalysts:** Q2 2026 earnings expected ~August 12, 2026 (guided $65-68m revenue); SkyWater acquisition close (Q2-Q3 2026); 256-qubit chip-based system commissioning targeted by end of Q2 2027.

**Risks:** Revenue is still tiny relative to a market cap in the tens of billions; commercial quantum advantage remains unproven; persistent, large cash burn; heavy reliance on continued capital markets access and stock-funded deals (dilution); execution risk integrating six-plus acquisitions simultaneously; extreme share-price volatility.

**Competitive position:** Largest pure-play quantum revenue base, but competes against superconducting players (Google, IBM, Rigetti), photonic/topological efforts (Microsoft, Quantum Computing Inc.), and deep-pocketed Big Tech programs.

**Valuation sense-check:** Market cap estimates ranged widely (~$10.6bn to ~$23.8bn) across May-June 2026 snapshots. Against ~$130m FY2025 revenue or ~$260-270m FY2026 guided revenue, P/S sits roughly in the 40x-90x range depending on which cap snapshot is used — illustrative only.

## QBTS — D-Wave Quantum

**What it does:** D-Wave runs a "dual-platform" strategy: legacy quantum annealing systems (Advantage2) for real-world optimization problems, sold as on-premises hardware or via the Leap cloud service; and, following its January 2026 acquisition of Quantum Circuits Inc. ($550m, ~$300m stock / ~$250m cash), an error-corrected gate-model platform using "dual-rail" superconducting qubits with built-in erasure detection. Named customers include Volkswagen, Mastercard, BBVA, and Florida Atlantic University.

**Latest results & financial health:** Q1 2026 revenue was ~$2.86m, down sharply from ~$15.0m in Q1 2025 — system-sales timing makes quarterly revenue lumpy. Net loss was ~$18.36m for the quarter. Commercial customers made up over 50% of 100+ total customers. The more notable number was bookings: a record ~$33.4m (up roughly 1,994-2,000% YoY), anchored by a $20m Advantage2 system sale to Florida Atlantic University and a $10m enterprise QCaaS deal with a Fortune 100 company; RPO/backlog reached ~$42.4m. Cash and marketable securities ~$588.4m as of March 31, 2026 (down from ~$635m at year-end 2025, reflecting the Quantum Circuits cash outlay), no debt disclosed. The stock-funded portion of the acquisition (~$300m in shares) is a direct dilution event.

**Bull thesis:** Real, paying commercial customers today (rare among pure-play quantum names) and large headline bookings growth; the Quantum Circuits deal gives D-Wave a second, technologically distinct horse (gate-model with erasure detection) alongside annealing; large cash buffer relative to burn rate.

**Catalysts:** Q2 2026 earnings expected ~August 6, 2026, with guidance for revenue "modestly up" from Q1 and most 2026 revenue weighted to H2; first-ever Investor Day held June 1, 2026 at the NYSE; continued Advantage2 usage growth (314% YoY cited).

**Risks:** Extremely lumpy, low-base revenue that depends on a handful of large system-sale "lumps" rather than recurring revenue; quarterly revenue actually fell YoY in the most recent print despite bookings hype; annealing's commercial relevance outside niche optimization use cases is debated versus gate-model/universal quantum computing; substantial dilution from the stock-funded acquisition; integration risk combining two very different qubit technologies/teams.

**Competitive position:** D-Wave is the only major vendor with a commercially mature annealing product, a genuine niche versus IBM, Google, Rigetti and IonQ, which all pursue gate-model approaches. The Quantum Circuits acquisition puts it in more direct competition with IBM, Google, and IonQ going forward, with far smaller resources than Big Tech competitors.

**Valuation sense-check:** Market cap snapshots ranged from roughly $5.3bn (June 10, 2026) to over $9bn (later June 2026). Against trailing revenue in the low-to-mid teens of millions (~$12-15m trailing), P/S ratios cited were extremely elevated (roughly 400x-700x+ in various snapshots) — illustrative only.

## CRCL — Circle Internet Group

**What it does:** Circle issues USDC, the second-largest US dollar stablecoin, and increasingly positions itself as blockchain financial infrastructure (cross-border payments, on-chain settlement, and its new ARC token/network). It makes money almost entirely from interest income on USDC reserves — cash and short-dated US Treasuries (~80% Treasuries/~20% cash, custodied at BNY Mellon, with BlackRock managing the Circle Reserve Fund money-market fund) — rather than from transaction fees on USDC itself.

**Latest results & financial health:** Q1 2026 (reported May 11, 2026): revenue and reserve income of ~$694m, up 20% YoY; net income from continuing operations of ~$55m (down 15% YoY); adjusted EBITDA ~$151m, up 24%. USDC in circulation reached ~$77.0bn (+28% YoY); on-chain USDC transaction volume hit ~$21.5tn in the quarter (+263% YoY). Q4 2025 revenue was ~$770m (+77% YoY) with USDC circulation of ~$75.3bn at year-end. Balance sheet carries no net debt. Stock jumped ~16% post-Q1 print.

**Bull thesis:** USDC has outgrown Tether's USDT for two straight years as regulated, US-domiciled stablecoins gain institutional favor post-GENIUS Act; Circle is building a broader settlement/network layer (ARC) with backing from BlackRock, ICE, Standard Chartered and others ($222m presale at a $3bn valuation); growing on-chain volumes suggest USDC is becoming embedded payments/settlement rail, not just a trading instrument.

**Catalysts ahead:** Q2 2026 earnings expected ~August 11, 2026; ARC network rollout and further institutional partnerships; continued GENIUS Act implementation and potential bank/fintech stablecoin entrants testing Circle's moat.

**Main risks:** Revenue is overwhelmingly interest income (~95% tied to rates) — Fed rate cuts would directly compress the reserve yield even as USDC float grows; intensifying competition from Tether, bank-issued stablecoins, and GENIUS Act-licensed entrants; valuation risk (rich multiple) leaves little room for a growth or rate disappointment.

**Competitive position:** Tether (USDT) remains far larger (~$190bn market cap) but USDC is the clear #2 and gaining share faster, particularly with institutions favoring regulatory compliance; combined, USDT and USDC are ~93% of stablecoin supply.

**Valuation sense-check:** Around $73-76/share in late June 2026 vs an IPO price of $31 (June 2025) — roughly +135-145% since IPO; market cap ~$19-20bn. P/S around 7.9x-8.5x, well above broader software-sector averages (~3.75x).

## KLAR — Klarna Group

**What it does:** Klarna is a Swedish buy-now-pay-later (BNPL) and digital banking platform, monetizing through merchant fees on BNPL transactions, "Pay in 4"/financing interest and fees, a growing direct-to-consumer banking/deposit business (~$13bn deposits as of Dec 2025), and an expanding ad/marketplace business layered on its app.

**Latest results & financial health:** Q1 2026 (reported May 14, 2026): revenue ~$1.0bn, up 44% YoY; GMV ~$33.7bn (+33% YoY); adjusted operating profit ~$68m (vs ~$3m a year ago); net income turned positive at ~$1m, a ~$100m YoY swing.
